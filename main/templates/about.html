Our Story

Welcome to the official website of Akinyele Local Government, the heart of progress and community in Ibadan, Oyo State. Akinyele is more than just a place on the map; it is a vibrant hub of culture, enterprise, and opportunity. Established in 1976, Akinyele was carved out of the former Ibadan North District Council and has since grown into a metropolitan area where tradition and modernity coexist harmoniously.

Akinyele Local Government Overview

Akinyele Local Government was carved out of the former Ibadan North District Council, comprising the present Akinyele and Ido Local Governments. The administrative seat was initially at the Health Centre, Oniyarin, Ibadan, later moved to an old house at Moniya, Ijaye Road Junction. The final split of the Local Government took place in 1976 and 1989 respectively.

Economic Activities

A good number of small, medium, and large-scale industries are established along Ibadan-Oyo road (the gateway to the Northern part of the country) and within the hinterland of the local government area.

The economic base of the local government is agriculture and commerce. The land is very fertile and falls within the forest and derived savannah zones. The people are predominantly farmers and artisans.

The presence of major institutions such as:

  • International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA)
  • Nigerian Institute of Social and Economic Research (NISER)
  • Federal School of Statistics

has attracted a diverse mix of people into the local government area, making the communities bustling and metropolitan.

Industrial and Commercial Development

The major economic hub is Ward Eight (Ojoo, Orogun, Sasa, Kajola, Gbanda, Kajorepo, and Akobo) with Moniya closely trailing behind.

Industries in Akinyele Local Government include:

Motor assembly plants, Hotels and guest houses, Palm oil and palm kernel processing, Petrol stations, Pure water plants, Block industries.

Some notable industries include:

Compole Electric Company, RCC Construction Company, Adisa Granite Company, Leventis Motor Assembly Plant, Fagol Engineering Company, PW Construction Company.

Major Markets

Markets for finished products are evenly distributed across the local government area, including:

    Ojoo, Moniya, Arulogun, Olunlosin, Ikereku, Elekuru, Beyioku, Sasa, Akinyele, Ogbogbologun, Ijaye, Olanla, Mele, Alabata, Onidundun, and many more.

    Health Facilities

    Akinyele Local Government is not lacking in the distribution of health facilities, supported by the Oyo State Government. These include maternity centres across the wards in the local government area.

About Us: Our History & Demographics

Akinyele Local Government was officially created in 1976 and is named after the late Olubadan, Isaac Babalola Akinyele. Our headquarters are situated in Moniya, serving as the administrative nerve center for the 12 wards that make up our local government area .

The population of Akinyele, last measured at 211,359, is predominantly made up of farmers and artisans. However, the presence of major institutions like the International Institute of Tropical Agriculture (IITA), Nigeria Institute of Social and Economic Research (NISER), and the Federal School of Statistics has attracted a diverse mix of people, making our communities bustling and metropolitan.

Economy: The Engine of Our Growth

The economy of Akinyele Local Government is anchored in agriculture and commerce. Our fertile land falls within the forest and derived savannah zones, making it ideal for cultivating a wide range of crops. Vibrant markets like Ojoo, Moniya, and Sasa are hubs of trade, while collaborations with institutions such as IITA enable improved crop yields and innovation.

The Inland Dry Port, Agro-processing Zone, and Agribusiness Industrial hubs (expected to generate over 100,000 jobs) are reshaping our economic future.
